Sydney Festival - Sydney, Australia

SydFest is a celebration of culture...

Each January, SydFest presents bold and memorable experiences that ignite, unite, and excite the city of Sydney. Each year presenting inspiring and ground-breaking new Australian art, with a program that celebrates the best work from Australias finest artists and companies.
More than any other cultural event, SydFest defines Sydney’s personality. SydFest is one of the most wonderful art festivals in the world.
For over four decades SydFest has presented international artists, commissioned works that have become Australian classics, opened new perspectives, and created a buzz like no other. SydFest is the ideal place to go for good music, extraordinary experiences, and to learn more about the incredible culture of Australia.

Top 5 places to eat

Australian hotel bar

Gloucester St, The Rocks NSW 2000, Australia

Under Australian hotel a wonderful pub makes itself a home. The food and drinks is amazing as well as affordable. It's right around the corner of the harbour bridge, and no more than a 10 minute walk from the Sydney opera house. The perfect place for either a lunch or dinner while at SydFest

Xi'an Eatery

183D Burwood Rd, Burwood NSW 2134, Australia

If you're in the mood for noodles, or just something to have on the go. Xian eatery serves up delicious chinese food, give it a try!

Four Frogs Crêperie

Gateway Sydney, 1 Macquarie Pl, Sydney NSW 2000, Australia

If you fancy something sweeter, then try visiting the four frogs Crêperie. Before or after you can take a short walk around the neighbourhood, or go down to the harbour.

Appetito Pizza Pasta Bar

Lower, Ground Floor/100 George St, The Rocks NSW 2000, Australia

Italian restaurant dishing up the classics amid sandstone walls, plus a covered dining terrace.

Phillip's Foote

101 George St, The Rocks NSW 2000, Australia

Traditional Australian steaks and pies in a 2-storey colonial building with courtyard seating.

Top 5 activities

Blue mountains

Take a guided tour of the blue mountains. There are many different options depending on what you wish to do. Most guides have stories about the legends of the mountains, and the beautiful nature makes this an amazing place for a hike.

Taronga Zoo

The zoo is amazing and easy to walk through. It's built on the side of a hill, and you take a gondola to the top, before walking down seeing different shows and amazing animals.

Hunter valley wine tasting

Hunter valley is home to many different wineries, and is famous for its amazing wines. Take a guided tour visiting different wineries around the area. There's something for everyone. Not only do you get amazing wine, there's also history in the buildings housing the wineries, and you can even see how the wine is made from grape to bottle.

Sydney opera house

The opera house in Sydney is an iconic building and a must see whilst in Sydney. There are guided tours everyday, simply visit the ticket booth and get a tour the same day. The architecture and history of the building is incredible, don't miss out on your chance to see this amazing place during your stay.

Bondi beach

The long white beach in Sydney is an amazing place to spend an afternoon away from the chaos of SydFest. Whether you wish to take a walk along the water or go for a refreshing dip in the sea, Bondi Beach is a beautiful place to relax. Or if you're here to experience even more of australian life, you can even learn to surf!
