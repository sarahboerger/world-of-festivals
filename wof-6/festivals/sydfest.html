Sydney Festival - Sydney, Australia

SydFest is something the cultural seeker would love...

Each January, SydFest presents bold and memorable experiences that ignite, unite, and excite the city of Sydney. Each year presenting inspiring and ground-breaking new Australian art, with a program that celebrates the best work from Australias finest artists and companies.
More than any other cultural event, SydFest defines Sydney’s personality. SydFest is one of the most wonderful art festivals in the world.
For over four decades SydFest has presented international artists, commissioned works that have become Australian classics, opened new perspectives, and created a buzz like no other. SydFest is the ideal place to go for good music, extraordinary experiences, and to learn more about the incredible culture of Australia.

Top 5 places to eat

Australian hotel bar

Gloucester St, The Rocks NSW 2000, Australia

Under Australian hotel a wonderful pub makes itself a home. The food and drinks are amazing as well as affordable. It's right around the corner of the harbour bridge, and no more than a 10 minute walk from the Sydney opera house. the perfect place for either a lunch or dinner while at SydFest

Xi'an Eatery

183D Burwood Rd, Burwood NSW 2134, Australia

If you're in the mood for noodles, or just something quick and easy. Xian eatery is a great place to go.

Four Frogs Crêperie

Gateway Sydney, 1 Macquarie Pl, Sydney NSW 2000, Australia

Do you wish for something slightly more sweet, then try visiting the four frogs Crêperie. Before or after you can take a short walk around the neighbourhood, or go down to the harbour.

Appetito Pizza Pasta Bar

Lower, Ground Floor/100 George St, The Rocks NSW 2000, Australia

Italian restaurant dishing up the classics amid sandstone walls, plus a covered dining terrace.

Phillip's Foote

101 George St, The Rocks NSW 2000, Australia

Traditional Australian steaks and pies in a 2-storey colonial building with courtyard seating.

Top 5 activities

Blue mountains

Take a guided tour of the blue mountains. There's many different options depending on what you wish to do. Most guides have stories about the legends of the mountains, and nature is an amazing place for a hike.

Taronga Zoo

The zoo is amazing and easy to walkthrough. It's built on the side of a hill, and you take a gondola to the top, before walking down seeing different shows and amazing animals.

Hunter valley wine tasting

Hunter valley is home to a lot of different wineries, and famous for its amazing wines. Take a guided tour visiting different wineries around the area. There's something for everyone. Not only do you get amazing wine, there's also history in the buildings housing the wineries, and you can get a tour and see how the wine is made from grape to bottle.

Sydney opera house

The opera house in Sydney is an iconic building and a must see. There's guided tours everyday, you can simply go to the ticket booth and get a tour the same day. The architecture and history of the building is something you can not miss when in Sydney.

Bondi beach

The long white beach in Sydney, is an amazing place to spend an afternoon away from the chaos of SydFest. Whether you wish to take a walk along the water or actually go swimming. If you have the time you could even learn to surf.
